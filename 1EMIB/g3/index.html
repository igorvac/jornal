Nindendo DS: a revolucionary portable gaming console.

The Nintendo DS is a handheld game console developed and produced by Nintendo, released in 2004.
It is visually distinguished by its open and close design, and the presence of two screens, the lower
one being touch sensitive. The system also has a built-in microphone, and supports wireless connection
via Wireless Local, allowing interaction between players within a small area
(9-30 meters, depending on conditions), or via Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection, which allows online multiplayer
with players from all over the world. The letters DS in the name were created to stand for both the expression
Dual Screen and Developers System, which refer to the innovative features of gameplay among game developers.
The system was dubbed Project Nitro during its development, just as the Wii was codenamed Revolution.
On March 2, 2006, Nintendo released the Nintendo DS Lite, a redesigned version of the Nintendo DS, in Japan.
Soon after, in June it was also released in North America and Europe. Later in 2009, the Nintendo DSi was released
with a new design, a nicer interface, specific apps for the new system, a webshop, 2 cameras, DS and DSi card slot,
SD card slot, larger screens , 133mhz processor (double the DS), 16MB RAM (four times the DS), among several other
improvements, however the new model had lost compatibility with Game Boy Advance titles and some original DS games that used the slot GBA
The Nintendo DS models combined have sold by 2016, 154.02 million units, making it the best-selling handheld
video game to date, and the second best-selling video game of all time behind only Sony's PlayStation 2. The Nintendo DS
lineup was replaced by the Nintendo 3DS family in 2011, which maintains compatibility with almost all Nintendo DS software,
except for some software that requires the GBA slot to use.

The history of Fortnite

Fortnite Battle Royale is a free-to-play electronic game in the battle royale genre developed and published by Epic Games. It was released as an early access title for Microsoft Windows, macOS, PlayStation 4 and Xbox One in September 2017, for iOS in April 2018, Nintendo Switch in June 2018, and an Android version in July 2020. Versions for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X are slated for late 2020. It's a Fortnite game mode from Epic, and a spin-off of Fortnite: Save the World, a co-op survival game with building elements. As in a battle royale game, Fortnite Battle Royale can be played with up to 99+ players on a map, alone, in pairs, or in squads of up to four players, trying to be the last player or party alive by killing other players or avoiding them. , while remaining within a safe zone that shrinks to avoid lethal damage from a storm blast. Players start with no intrinsic advantages and need to search for weapons and equipment and materials to gain an advantage over their opponents. The game adds the Fortnite building element; Players can split most objects in the game world to obtain resources that can be used to build fortifications as part of their strategy. The game features limited cross-platform modes across platforms. The idea of ​​developing Fortnite Battle Royale came after the release of PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds, which broke numerous player records throughout 2017. Seeing its rapid growth and commercial success, Epic Games saw the opportunity to create its own battle royale mode. . Originally released as a paid game mode, Epic has released a dedicated version of the game offered as free-to-play funded by microtransactions, sharing in-game currency with the main game Fortnite, which allows players to buy or earn cosmetics and items. that customize their skins. As of July 2018, the game has garnered over 125 million players, and is estimated to have won more than $1 billion in microtransactions. Fortnite Battle Royale has become a cultural phenomenon, with Fortnite streams breaking audience records, and with numerous celebrities and athletes affirming their appreciation of the game.
